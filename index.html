"Good design is obvious. Great design is transparent." ~ Joe Sparano


Building software is not usually a terribly difficult task. Any idea can be thought of, planned, created and then only minimally tested before sent out to world for it to be broken, patched and broken again until the developer has torn their eyelids out and the user is left frustrated. However, building great software requires careful planning, an excellent team or individual and deliberate testing before reaching a production level.

I am inspired frequently by the elegance of a track bicycle. For those unfamilar, it has all the similarity of a normal bicycle except a fixed cog and a single chainring in the front. The design is simple on a visual level, yet it is robust in the most hidden ways. The bearings in the wheels and bottom bracket (bearings which allow the crank to spin) are seated neatly to allow as frictionless motion as possible allowing it's rider to roar around a banked track at upwards of 40 miles per hour. The alumninum, steel or carbon frame has been shaped to hide from the wind, only exposing itself in the most minimal ways.

Good software should be like a track bicycle. Robust yet simple. Fast yet delicate.
